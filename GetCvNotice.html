Get your CV noticed online

To get your CV noticed, you should include a cover letter with every one of your applications. Whether you are applying via a job website or direct email, write a friendly and professional paragraph to introduce yourself and explain why your CV is worth reading.

Make sure your CV looks professional

A logical structure with clearly defined sections and bold headings are essential. There should be no typos or grammatical errors in your CV, double check and check again for mistakes.

Create an impact upon opening

Highlight talents that are relevant to the position or company, whatever your passion, use your unique qualities and experiences to make yourself stand out.

Make sure your CV shows the value you can add to the Company

Employers want to hire people who can? Make an impact. Write a tailored CV that shows a distinctive, results-driven professional.

Short and sharp

Make sure your CV is no more than 3 pages long and that it is short, sharp and to-the-point. Interview Preparation
